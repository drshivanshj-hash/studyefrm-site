Core Innovation

The Vantage-Point System

One concept. Five clinical angles. Different language every time. Not pattern recognition — subconscious encoding. The exam rotates vantage points deliberately. So does this system.

Why standard preparation fails

When a mock uses the same wording, same demographics, same diagnostic structure — your brain switches from concept retrieval to pattern recognition. Mock scores inflate. Exam performance drops. You see "PCOS + AMH 8 + IVF" and click antagonist protocol without thinking. That is recall, not reasoning.

Reproductive Endocrinology

PCOS · POI · Thyroid · Prolactin · Ovulation disorders

D1
QAMH primarily reflects which follicle pool?
Antral follicle pool (small antral follicles, 2–10mm). Secreted by granulosa cells. Does not reflect primordial reserve. Does not fluctuate with cycle day — unlike FSH. ESHRE: AMH is the preferred single marker of ovarian reserve for ART prognosis.
QTSH threshold for IVF — ESHRE 2023?
<2.5 mIU/L before ART. Levothyroxine if TSH 2.5–4.0 with positive TPO antibodies. If TSH >4.0 — treat regardless of antibody status. Most commonly tested thyroid threshold in EFRM.
QRotterdam criteria — how many of 3 features for PCOS diagnosis?
2 of 3. (1) Oligo/anovulation, (2) Biochemical/clinical hyperandrogenism, (3) Polycystic ovarian morphology (≥20 follicles per ovary OR volume >10ml). Rotterdam 2003 — still the international standard per ESHRE 2023.
QFSH threshold defining POI?
FSH >25 IU/L on two occasions at least 4 weeks apart, before age 40. ESHRE POI guideline. Must be combined with ≥4 months menstrual disturbance. Karyotype mandatory in all POI <35.
QFirst-line pharmacological OI in PCOS — ESHRE/ASRM 2023?
Letrozole. ESHRE/ASRM PCOS 2023: letrozole recommended over clomiphene as first-line OI. Higher LBR, lower multiple pregnancy rate, better endometrial response. Dose: 2.5–7.5mg days 2–6.

Infertility & MAR

IVF · ICSI · Stimulation · OHSS · FET protocols

D2
QPOSEIDON Group 3 definition?
Age <35 + predicted poor ovarian response (AMH <1.2 ng/ml OR AFC <5). POSEIDON 2016: Groups 1–2 = unexpected poor response; Groups 3–4 = predicted poor response. Group 4 = same but age ≥35.
QGnRH agonist trigger — most important advantage?
Significantly lower OHSS risk. Endogenous LH surge — shorter duration, lower amplitude than exogenous hCG. Antagonist protocol only. High responders (AFC >20): GnRHa trigger + freeze-all = ESHRE-recommended OHSS prevention strategy.
QBest strategy to reduce multiple pregnancy in IVF?
Elective single embryo transfer (eSET). ESHRE embryo transfer guideline 2023. NOT PGT-A. NOT blastocyst culture alone. Cumulative LBR with eSET + FET is equivalent to DET. Multiple pregnancy is the most preventable ART complication.
QNatural cycle FET vs HRT-FET — preferred in ovulatory women?
Natural cycle FET. ESHRE FET guideline 2022. NEET RCT (Lancet 2023): NC-FET shows higher LBR and lower placental complications vs HRT-FET. Use HRT-FET only when NC-FET is not possible.
QProgesterone threshold for freeze-all conversion?
≥1.5 ng/ml on day of trigger. Premature P rise ≥1.5 ng/ml associated with reduced implantation in fresh cycles. Freeze-all recommended. Impairs endometrial receptivity — does NOT affect embryo quality.

Laboratory — Embryology & Genetics

Fertilisation · Embryo grading · PGT · Cryobiology

D3
QGardner grading — what does the number represent?
Expansion stage (1–6). 1=early blast, 2=blast, 3=full blast, 4=expanded (thinned zona), 5=hatching, 6=hatched. Then ICM (A/B/C) and TE (A/B/C). 4AA = expanded + excellent ICM + excellent TE.
QWhich marker most strongly predicts embryo implantation?
Embryo ploidy (euploid status). NOT morphology alone. NOT endometrial thickness. Chromosomally normal embryo has highest implantation potential regardless of morphological grade. Key EFRM trap question.
QVitrification survival rate benchmark — ESHRE Vienna Consensus?
≥80% minimum, ≥90% benchmark for quality centres. Istanbul Consensus: survival = >50% intact cells + evidence of re-expansion. Re-expansion expected within 2–4 hours post-thaw.
QMost common cause of cleavage-stage embryo arrest?
Aneuploidy (chromosomal imbalance). Embryo competence dominates — not lab conditions. This is why Day 3 arrested embryos have very low success rates. EFRM exam rule: embryo competence > culture conditions.
QEGA in human embryos — when does it occur?
4–8 cell stage (Day 3) for major EGA. Embryo transitions from maternal mRNA to embryonic genome control. This is why Day 3 arrest is clinically significant. Preimplantation embryos rely on maternal RNA until EGA.

Andrology

Spermatogenesis · Azoospermia · Sperm retrieval · Genetics

D4
QAZF microdeletion — which region predicts no sperm on TESE?
AZFa and AZFb deletions. AZFa = Sertoli Cell Only. AZFb = maturation arrest. Both = no sperm on microTESE. AZFc = variable, sperm found in 50–70%. CFTR = CBAVD (obstructive azoospermia, normal spermatogenesis).
QWHO 2021 reference — sperm morphology (Kruger strict)?
≥4% normal forms. WHO 2021 lower reference limits: Volume ≥1.4ml, Concentration ≥16M/ml, Total motility ≥42%, Progressive motility ≥30%, Morphology ≥4%. Isolated teratozoospermia is NOT a standard ESHRE indication for ICSI.
QPreferred sperm retrieval for non-obstructive azoospermia (NOA)?
Microdissection TESE (microTESE). Higher retrieval rates than conventional TESE/TESA in NOA. Targets focal spermatogenesis. PESA/TESA reserved for obstructive azoospermia. Klinefelter: microTESE achieves sperm in ~50%.
QKlinefelter syndrome — karyotype and fertility implication?
47XXY — most common cause of primary hypogonadism and azoospermia. microTESE possible in ~50%. ICSI with PGT-A recommended (increased sex chromosome aneuploidy in offspring). Do NOT start testosterone before sperm retrieval.
QSperm DNA fragmentation — clinical threshold?
DFI >30% — significantly reduced IVF/ICSI success. ESHRE: not routine screening, indicated in unexplained infertility, RPL, repeated ART failure. ICSI may partially overcome DFI for fertilisation but not embryo development.

Reproductive Surgery

Endometriosis · Fibroids · Uterine anomalies · Ectopic

D5
QEndometrioma surgery before IVF — ESHRE recommendation?
NOT recommended routinely before IVF. ESHRE endometriosis 2022: cystectomy does not improve LBR AND reduces ovarian reserve. Surgery for: rapidly growing, >3cm with poor access, persistent pain, malignancy suspicion.
QHydrosalpinx effect on IVF — recommended management?
Reduces IVF LBR by ~50% — salpingectomy or proximal tubal occlusion before IVF. Hydrosalpinx fluid is embryotoxic AND impairs endometrial receptivity. Salpingectomy preferred per NICE and ESHRE. Cochrane confirms significantly improved LBR.
QWhich uterine anomaly most commonly causes RPL?
Septate uterus. Most common Müllerian anomaly (35%). Associated with highest RPL rate (60–65% pregnancy loss). ESHRE RPL: hysteroscopic metroplasty significantly reduces miscarriage rate. ESGE/ESHRE Class U2.
QASRM Stage IV endometriosis — score?
Score >40. ASRM revised classification: Stage I (1–5), II (6–15), III (16–40), IV (>40). Stage IV = dense adhesions, bilateral endometriomas, obliteration of POD. NOTE: AFS score does NOT predict fertility outcome reliably.
QAsherman syndrome — gold standard diagnosis and treatment?
Hysteroscopy — gold standard for both diagnosis AND treatment. ESHRE: complete adhesiolysis under hysteroscopic vision. Post-treatment oestrogen therapy to promote endometrial regeneration. Second-look hysteroscopy 2–4 weeks in severe cases.

Early Pregnancy & Implantation

RIF · RPL · Ectopic · βhCG interpretation

D6
QESHRE definition of Recurrent Implantation Failure (RIF)?
Failure to achieve clinical pregnancy after ≥3 good quality embryos in ≥3 fresh or frozen cycles in a woman under 40. ESHRE RIF guideline 2023. NOT the same as RPL. Most adjuncts (ERA, endometrial scratch, immunologicals) — insufficient evidence for routine use.
QExpected βhCG rise at 48 hours — viable IUP?
≥66% rise in 48 hours. NOT "doubling" — minimum 66% is evidence-based. Rise <53% = non-viable. 53–66% = indeterminate — repeat. Discriminatory zone: ~1500–2000 IU/L. Single βhCG is NEVER diagnostic.
QMost common cause of RPL?
Embryonic chromosomal abnormality (~50% of cases). APS is the most important treatable cause. Thrombophilia panel: lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin IgG/IgM, anti-β2GP1 — on 2 occasions, 12 weeks apart.
QFirst-line treatment for APS in RPL?
Low-dose aspirin + LMWH. ESHRE and RCOG: combination therapy reduces miscarriage from ~90% to ~75%. Start aspirin pre-conceptually, add LMWH once pregnancy confirmed. Continue to 34 weeks minimum.
QMTX criteria for ectopic pregnancy?
Haemodynamically stable + βhCG <5000 IU/L + adnexal mass <35mm + no cardiac activity + no rupture. NICE NG126. Single-dose MTX 50mg/m². Repeat βhCG day 4 and 7 — if <15% fall, consider repeat or surgery.

Fertility Preservation

Oncofertility · Social freezing · Transgender FP · Cryobiology

D7
QHighest gonadotoxic chemotherapy agents?
Alkylating agents — cyclophosphamide, chlorambucil, busulfan. Direct DNA damage to primordial follicles — irreversible. ESHRE FP: strongest indication for urgent FP. Radiation >2Gy to ovaries also highly gonadotoxic.
QWhen is ovarian tissue cryopreservation preferred over oocyte freezing?
Prepubertal girls, or when stimulation not possible. Oocyte freezing requires 10–14 days. Not possible prepubertal or when chemotherapy cannot be delayed. ESHRE: ovarian tissue banking no longer experimental for female FP.
QTransgender male FP — timing relative to testosterone therapy?
Before initiating testosterone OR after cessation. Testosterone suppresses ovarian function but does NOT eliminate fertility permanently. Stimulation possible after cessation (6–8 weeks for menstrual resumption). Transabdominal USS monitoring preferred.
QRecommended age for social egg freezing — ESHRE?
Before age 35, ideally before 37. ESHRE FP 2020: oocyte yield and quality decline significantly after 35. <35: expect 15–20+ MII. After 40: poor outcomes from own eggs — counsel toward donor. Minimum 8–10 MII for acceptable cumulative LBR.
QGnRH agonist co-treatment during chemotherapy — evidence for ovarian protection?
Controversial — NOT recommended as sole FP method. ESHRE FP: may reduce ovarian failure in some studies but does NOT reliably protect primordial follicle pool. ASCO 2018: insufficient evidence. Should NOT replace established FP methods.

Adolescent Female — Irregular Menses, Hyperandrogenism, Metabolic Features

A 17-year-old presenting to gynaecology OPD. This case illustrates the single most common diagnostic error made in adolescent PCOS — and the examiner question most candidates cannot answer cleanly.

Station 2 Paediatric Gynaecology Decision ESHRE 2023 Adolescent PCOS

Clinical scenario: 17-year-old female. Menarche age 12. Menses occur only with progesterone withdrawal or after a full 21-day OCP course. BMI 33.3. Ferriman-Gallwey score 10. Acanthosis nigricans. USS: bilateral polycystic ovarian morphology. Fasting insulin 25 mIU/ml. Testosterone 0.9 ng/ml. Day 2 FSH/LH: 7/7. TSH 1.3. Prolactin normal.

Clinical Question

In a 17-year-old with menstrual irregularity, clinical hyperandrogenism, and polycystic morphology on USS, what is the correct diagnostic framework — and where does the standard clinical approach most commonly go wrong?

⚠️ The Common Error

Applying adult Rotterdam criteria directly to an adolescent. This is the most frequently made diagnostic error in this station. Clinicians see polycystic morphology and two Rotterdam features and reach a PCOS diagnosis — without recognising that Rotterdam was not validated within 8 years of menarche. PCOM is present in up to 40% of adolescents without PCOS.

✅ ESHRE 2023 — Correct Reasoning

✓ Hyperandrogenism — FG 10, testosterone 0.9 ng/ml (elevated)
✓ Ovulatory dysfunction — cycles only with withdrawal
✓ Metabolic features — HOMA-IR elevated, acanthosis
This patient meets adolescent PCOS criteria — 2 criteria met.

Next-Step Gaps — What Candidates Commonly Miss

⚠️ OGTT not done — fasting insulin alone insufficient for HOMA-IR
⚠️ 17-OHP not checked — must exclude NCCAH before PCOS diagnosis
⚠️ DHEAS, SHBG, free androgen index not measured
⚠️ OCP for only 3 cycles — ESHRE recommends 6–12 months minimum
⚠️ Spironolactone without contraception counselling (teratogenic)
⚠️ No VTE risk assessment for OCP in obese adolescent
